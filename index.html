EST. LOUISIANA LAGNIAPPE SYRUPS · BITTERS · DRY FRUIT made with a little extra care

lagniappe / ‘lan-yap / noun

Down here, lagniappe means a little something extra. The thirteenth beignet. The oyster the shucker slides you while you wait. It's not a bonus you pay for and it's not a gimmick. It's generosity worked into the thing itself instead of tacked on after. That's how we make everything. The extra step isn't extra to us. It's just how it's done.

What we make

Three things, made properly

Cocktail Syrups

Real sugar, real fruit, real spice, cooked in small batches and bottled fresh. Louisiana cane sugar, citrus from down the river, honey from folks we know by name. A syrup should taste like the thing on the label. Ours do.

Bitters

Whole botanicals, extracted slow, balanced by palate instead of by spreadsheet. Bitters are the seasoning of a drink, a dash or two that pulls everything into focus. Twenty years behind the bar taught us exactly where that focus lives.

Dried Fruit

Citrus wheels and garnishes that finish a drink the way it deserves. Sliced thin, dried slow, ready when you are. It's the last ninety seconds of care, done ahead of time so you don't have to.

Our story

Twenty years behind the bar taught us a few things

Lagniappe Provisions started in a kitchen in Algiers Point, with a stovetop full of test batches and a growing suspicion that the syrups behind most bars weren't good enough. What began as making better ingredients for our own drinks turned into recipes worth sharing, then recipes worth bottling. Merry and Jason built the company on a simple idea. Source close to home, make it in small batches, and test everything on real drinks before it goes in a bottle. Alma Plantation cane sugar. Becnel Farms citrus. Honey from The B Farm. Everything within a day's drive of the kitchen where it all started. We named the company after the thing we believe in most. The little extra, given freely, built in from the start.
